Section 12.3 Dot Products

Two forms of the Dot Product

Dot product Form #1

Given two nonzero vectors uuu and vvv in two or three dimensions, their dot product is

u⋅v=∣u∣∣v∣cos⁡θ\begin{aligned} u\cdot v = |u| |v| \cos \th \end{aligned} u⋅v=∣u∣∣v∣cosθ​

where θ\thθ is the angle between uuu and vvv with 0⩽θ⩽π0 \les \th \les \pi0⩽θ⩽π. If u=0u=0u=0 or v=0v=0v=0, then u⋅vu\cdot vu⋅v, and θ\thθ is undefined.
The dot product of two vectors is itself a scalar. Two special cases immediately asrise:

  • uuu and vvv are parallel (θ=0\th = 0θ=0 or θ=π\th = \piθ=π) if only if u⋅v=±∣u∣∣v∣u \cdot v = \pm |u||v|u⋅v=±∣u∣∣v∣.
  • uuu and vvv are perpendicular (θ=π/2\th = \pi/2θ=π/2) if and only if u⋅v=0u \cdot v = 0u⋅v=0

Dot product Form #2

Given two vectors u=⟨u1,u2,u3⟩u=\la u_1, u_2, u_3 \rau=⟨u1​,u2​,u3​⟩ and v=⟨v1,v2,v3⟩v=\la v_1, v_2, v_3 \rav=⟨v1​,v2​,v3​⟩,

u⋅v=u1v1+u3v3+u3v3\begin{aligned} u \cdot v = u_1 v_1 + u_3 v_3 + u_3 v_3 \end{aligned} u⋅v=u1​v1​+u3​v3​+u3​v3​​

If uuu and vvv are both nonzero, then

cos⁡θ=u1v1+u3v3+u3v3∣u∣∣v∣=u⋅v∣u∣∣v∣\begin{aligned} \cos\th = \frac{u_1 v_1 + u_3 v_3 + u_3 v_3}{|u||v|} = \frac{u\cdot v}{|u||v|} \end{aligned} cosθ=∣u∣∣v∣u1​v1​+u3​v3​+u3​v3​​=∣u∣∣v∣u⋅v​​

Orthogonal Projections

(Orthogonal) Projection of uuu onto vvv

The orthogonal projection of uuu onto vvv, denoted projvu\text{proj}_v uprojv​u, where v≠0v \ne 0v≠0 is

projvu=∣u∣cos⁡θ(v∣v∣)\begin{aligned} \text{proj}_v u = |u| \cos \th (\frac{v}{|v|}) \end{aligned} projv​u=∣u∣cosθ(∣v∣v​)​

projvu=scalvu(v∣v∣)=(u⋅vv⋅v)v\begin{aligned} \text{proj}_v u = \text{scal}_v u (\frac{v}{|v|}) = (\frac{u\cdot v}{v\cdot v})v \end{aligned} projv​u=scalv​u(∣v∣v​)=(v⋅vu⋅v​)v​

scalvu=∣u∣cos⁡θ=u⋅v∣v∣\begin{aligned} \text{scal}_v u = |u|\cos \th = \frac{u\cdot v}{|v|} \end{aligned} scalv​u=∣u∣cosθ=∣v∣u⋅v​​
