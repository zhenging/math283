Section 12.9 Curvature and Normal Vectors

Curvature

If a small increment in arc length Δs\Delta sΔs along the curve results in a large change in the direction of TTT, the curve is turning quickly over that interval and we say it has a large curvature ( Figure 12.102a). If a small increment Δs\Delta sΔs in arc length results in a small change in the direction of TTT, the curve is turning slowly over that interval and it has a small curvature (Figure 12.102b). The magnitude of the rate at which the direction of TTT changes with respect to arc length is the curvature of the curve.

Let rrr describe a smooth parameterized curve. If sss denotes arc length and T=r′∣r′∣T = \dfrac{r'}{|r'|}T=∣r′∣r′​ is the unit tangent vector, the curvature is κ(s)=∣dTds∣\kappa(s) = |\dfrac{dT}{ds}|κ(s)=∣dsdT​∣.

Principal Unit Normal Vector

Let rrr describe a smooth parameterized curve. The principal unit normal vector at a point PPP on the curve at which κ≠0\kappa \ne 0κ≠0 is

N(s)=dT/ds∣dT/ds∣=1κdTds\begin{aligned} N(s) = \frac{dT/ds}{|dT/ds|} = \frac{1}{\kappa} \frac{dT}{ds} \end{aligned} N(s)=∣dT/ds∣dT/ds​=κ1​dsdT​​

In practice, we use the equivalent formula

N(t)=dT/dt∣dT/dt∣\begin{aligned} N(t) = \frac{dT/dt}{|dT/dt|} \end{aligned} N(t)=∣dT/dt∣dT/dt​​

evaluated at value of ttt corresponding to PPP.

Proerties of the Principal Unit NOrmal Vector

Let rrr describe a smooth parameterized curve with unit tangent vector TTT and principal unit normal vector NNN.
1. TTT and NNN are orthogonal at all points of the curve; that is, T(t)⋅N(t)=0T(t) \cdot N(t) = 0T(t)⋅N(t)=0 at all points where NNN is defined.
2. The principal unit normal vector points to the inside of the curve - in the direction that the curve is turning.

The Binormal Vector and Torsion

Define the unit binormal vector B=T×NB = T \times NB=T×N. BBB is orthogonal to both TTT and NNN. Becase TTT and NNN are unit vector, BBB is also a unit vector. T,NT, NT,N and BBB form a right handed coordinate system that changes its orientation as we move along the curve. This coordinate system is ofen called the TNB frame.

The rate at which the curve CCC twists out of the plane determined by TTT and NNN is the rate at which BBB changes as we move along CCC, which is dBdS\dfrac{dB}{dS}dSdB​.

(1) - dBds\dfrac{dB}{ds}dsdB​ is orthogonal to both TTT and dNds\dfrac{dN}{ds}dsdN​, because it is the cross product of TTT and dNds\dfrac{dN}{ds}dsdN​.
(2) - dBds\dfrac{dB}{ds}dsdB​ is orthogonal to BBB. (Theorem 12.8)
(3) - From (1) and (2), dBds\dfrac{dB}{ds}dsdB​ is orthogonal to both TTT and BBB, so it must be parallel to NNN. We write

dBds=−τN\begin{aligned} \frac{dB}{ds} = -\tau N \end{aligned} dsdB​=−τN​
