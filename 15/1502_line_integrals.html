Scalar Line Integrals in the Plane

Suppose the scalar-valued function fff is defined on the smooth curve C:r(s)=⟨x(s),y(s)⟩C:r(s) = \la x(s), y(s) \raC:r(s)=⟨x(s),y(s)⟩, parameterized by the arc length sss. The line integral of fff over CCC is

∫Cf(x(s),y(s))ds=lim⁡Δ→0∑k=1bf(x(sk∗),y(sk∗))\begin{aligned} \int_C f(x(s), y(s)) ds = \lim_{\Delta \to 0} \sum_{k=1}^b f(x(s_k^*), y(s_k^*)) \end{aligned} ∫C​f(x(s),y(s))ds=Δ→0lim​k=1∑b​f(x(sk∗​),y(sk∗​))​

provided this limit exists over all partitions of CCC. When the limit exists, fff is said to be integrable on CCC.
