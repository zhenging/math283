Gradient Fields and Potential Functions

A vector field defined as the gradient of a scalar-valued function φ\varphiφ is called a gradient field and the function φ\varphiφis called a potential function.

Equipotential Curves and Surfaces

The level curve of a potential function are called equipotential curves (curves on which the potential function is constant.) The idea also apply to vector field in \R^3 in which case the vector field is orthogonal to the equipotential surfaces.

EXAMPLE 5 Equipotential curves

The equipotential curves for the potential function φ(x,y)=(x2−y2)/2\varphi(x, y) = (x^2 - y^2) / 2φ(x,y)=(x2−y2)/2.
a. Find the gradient field associated with φ\varphiφ and verify that the gradient field is orthogonal to the equipotential curve at (2,1)(2, 1)(2,1).
b. Verify that the vector field F=∇φF = \nabla \varphiF=∇φ is orthogonal to the equipotential curves at all points (x,y)(x, y)(x,y).

Solution

b. In general, the line tangent to the equipotential curve at (x,y)(x, y)(x,y) is parallel to the vector ⟨y,x⟩\la y, x \ra⟨y,x⟩, while the vector field at that points is F=⟨x,−y⟩F = \la x, -y \raF=⟨x,−y⟩. The vector field and the tangent vectors are orthogonal because ⟨y,x⟩⋅⟨x,−y⟩=0\la y, x \ra \cdot \la x, -y \ra = 0⟨y,x⟩⋅⟨x,−y⟩=0.
